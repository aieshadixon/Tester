Afrofuturism
Let's talk about Afrofuturism. Throughout history, black people across the diaspora have been the pioneers and inventors of many great things. Afrofuturism is one of those great things. It is a genre that intertwines black culture with science fiction, technology, and history. This fantastical combination is portrayed through literature, music, and art - specifically visual art. It has its roots in black activism and 1980s technoculture. The term Afrofuturism was first coined by Mark Dery in 1993. A mix of the past, present, and future. I am captivated by the sheer greatness that the afrofuturistic aesthetic exudes. It extraordinarily showcases the imagination and creativity of black people. Black culture is rich with diversity, so why not celebrate it in such a way? It is important because it is a genre solely based on the African diaspora. There is also an element of escapism. Given the deep-rooted struggles and hardships of black people throughout history and modern society, Afrofuturism creates a place for freedom and liberation. I am provided with a renewed sense of love for my heritage.
